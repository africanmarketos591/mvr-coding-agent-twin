# Research Protocols — What to Research (Never Ask)

Law 2 corollary: asking the user a researchable fact is a logged defect. This file defines what "researchable" means per run. Execute BEFORE the charter; cite findings with sources in the charter's research appendix.

## General High-Context Protocol (all archetypes, in order)

1. **Incumbency scan** — who already does the stated job in this geography? Named products, age, scale, backers. (The S16B exhibit: a 10-year incumbent with 15,000 schools was one search away; the naive build missed it.)
2. **Rails ownership** — who owns the underlying rails (payments, logistics, identity, distribution)? Riding rails is a wedge; fighting rails is a war — classify which one the idea implies.
3. **Guardian topology** — pull `category_playbook(archetype)`: guardian tiers, veto holders. Then research WHO those are locally, by name where public (regulator, association, union, council).
4. **Permission structure** — licensing/registration reality for this category and country (tiers, fees, timelines). Never approximate license = permission; they are different layers (a license is necessary, relational permission is decisive).
5. **Category failure modes** — playbook `failure_modes` + research post-mortems of the named category in the named market. Who died, and of what?
6. **Reputation channels** — where does word-of-mouth actually run for this stakeholder set (associations, WhatsApp/community structures, radio, market days)? You are designing for the whisper network's carry.
7. **Counterparty existence check** — do the assumed counterparties exist at assumed density (e.g., "5,000 SACCOs" — verify count, health, digitization)?
8. **Eclipse test (angle 8)** — existing tools, incumbent-feature risk, model-update erasure, generation-only value. Verdict in writing.
9. **Unit reality** — price points, margins, fee tolerances actually observed in this market (not US analogues). Cite local figures or mark unknown.
10. **Passport fit** — map surviving build variants against the operator's inferred assets; kill variants this operator cannot carry (angle 9).
11. **Evidence bill** — from playbook + sparring output: what evidence would be required before pilot / before scale. Goes in the charter verbatim.
12. **Abstention honesty** — list what could not be established and mark it UNKNOWN in the charter. Unknown is a finding; a guess dressed as a finding is fraud.

## fintech_platform Specialization (first calibrated archetype)

- Licensing tiers & fees for the specific country (e.g., UG: NPS Act tiers via BoU; KE: CBK tier capital + fees; NG: CBN PSSP/PTSP/MMO capital blocks) — verify current figures each run; regulatory numbers stale fast and stale figures are trust-fatal.
- Float & agent-network reality: who owns cash-in/cash-out where the users are; agent liquidity patterns.
- Trust intermediaries: SACCOs, chamas/ROSCAs, cooperatives, associations — the actual credit/trust layer the product must join, not bypass.
- Mobile-money penetration vs bank penetration for the target segment (design for the dominant rail).
- Fraud/scam history in the category and market — inherited distrust is a design input (what reassurance artifacts must exist in v1?).
- KYC/data rules: consent, retention, localization requirements — the instrumentation layer must be born compliant (privacy envelopes are schema-enforced, not optional).

## Adding archetypes
Copy the general protocol, add: (a) the archetype's playbook pull, (b) 4–6 category-specific checks, (c) the market-data sources that must be re-verified per run. Certify the archetype's instrumentation mapping (named human) before its telemetry is treated as evidence.
