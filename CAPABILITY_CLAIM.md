# Capability Claim And Boundary

## What is earned today

On the published 12-case controlled benchmark, the same class of host agent with the MVR Twin scored 89.8 versus 43.6 without it, a +46.2 Viability Grounding Score delta. Treatment had zero fabrication and zero false-calibration incidents, and all 12 treatment receipts verified against the kernel ledger.

The earned claim is:

> A strong coding agent plus the MVR Twin showed materially better pre-code market-viability judgment and calibration honesty on this controlled African/high-context benchmark than the agent alone.

The deterministic spine authorizes finite claim classes. The local claim gate enforces those finite classes. The build-constraint layer preserves charter constraints, detects stale or weakened contracts, and runs a lexical tripwire. Semantic review covers every first-party non-binary text file under the declared product paths, while opaque files are disclosed and hash-bound. The result is reviewer-attested, not deterministic proof. Host-model self-review supports local accountability but does not count as independent assurance.

## What is not claimed

- The Twin is not itself a Fable-class reasoner.
- A clear lexical tripwire does not prove forbidden behavior is absent.
- Model semantic review is not un-promptable kernel authority.
- Binary behavior is not semantically reviewed merely because its bytes are hash-bound.
- The +46.2 result is not yet peer-audited science; arm separation lacks immutable run identities and full orchestration transcripts.
- No global capability claim exists outside calibrated African/high-context markets.

## The future Fable-class acceptance bar

"Fable-class in its own domain" is reserved until all of these are met on the same frozen evaluation:

1. At least 50 blind cases across at least three market families.
2. Host-alone, host-plus-doctrine, and host-plus-full-Twin ablation arms.
3. Symmetric verdict records and an independent judge; semantic code-constraint scoring must use a reviewer distinct from the code-producing host.
4. Opaque agent IDs, exact prompts, timestamps, and artifact hashes sufficient to reconstruct arm separation.
5. Four measured axes: verdict correctness, semantic code-constraint detection, evidence discipline, and calibration honesty.
6. The host-plus-Twin configuration is not materially worse than the reference frontier reviewer on any axis and materially improves at least one axis over host-alone.
7. False-positive and false-negative rates are published with misses, costs, and latency.

Until then, use the controlled-beta claim above. Capability is earned by the record, not by the adjective.
