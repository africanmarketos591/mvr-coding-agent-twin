## PIVOT (plain language, before the charter)
A "500-person waitlist" is a signature, not demand — under the Twin's rules a founder's own traction claim enters at 0.30 weight until field-attested, and an email list has never once predicted that a social graph will actually light up. Horizontal student "social networks" almost universally die because the students you want are already inside WhatsApp, Instagram, Telegram and TikTok, and a cold-start network has no reason to reach critical mass against them. We keep your real asset — access to Nairobi students who want to share notes and find events — but redirect from "build a social network" to "own one acute job on one campus," which is winnable.

## 1. The idea as received
> A dedicated social network for Nairobi university students to connect, share notes, and find events. Founder: "I already have 500 students on a waitlist, demand is proven."

## 2. What we researched
- **Founder claim priced at 0.30:** a waitlist is self-reported intent, not attested behaviour. "Demand is proven" is exactly the persuasive-but-cheap conviction the Twin discounts until a field pack corroborates it.
- **Eclipse (angle 8):** the incumbent is not a startup — it's WhatsApp/Instagram/Telegram/TikTok where students *already are*. A new horizontal social app must overcome switching + cold-start simultaneously. This is the classic social-network graveyard.
- **What survives:** single-purpose utility inside ONE campus (notes exchange, past-papers, events board) can win because it does a job the general platforms do badly and needs only a bounded network to be useful.
- **Guardian surface (kernel: macro/meso/micro):** soft/none — no licence. Risk is cold-start + eclipse, not permission.

## 4. Who you are in this build (0.30 inference)
No passport; the waitlist is the only asset and it sits at 0.30. It CAN carry a single-campus utility launch (a warm list is enough to seed one job), but not a general social graph.

## 5. THE BUILD (fitted smallest wedge)
- **Build:** ONE acute job on ONE campus — a **notes / past-papers exchange tied to the exam calendar** (or an events board, pick the one the waitlist actually asked for), seeded from the existing 500.
- **For:** students of one named university/faculty.
- **Distributed through:** the existing waitlist + campus WhatsApp groups (ride the incumbent, don't fight it).
- **Explicitly NOT building:** a general "social network," profiles/feeds/DMs, multi-campus rollout, monetization. Connection graphs are the thing that dies; a shared utility is the thing that sticks.
- **What a demo will NOT prove:** that the waitlist converts to weekly active use. A signup ≠ a returning user.

## 6. Redirect
Died on: waitlist ≠ demand (0.30) + total eclipse by existing platforms + social cold-start. Adjacent build: single-job, single-campus utility. Smallest pilot: activate the 500 around one exam season.
